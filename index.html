AI Foundations Badge Course Overview

Artificial Intelligence is rapidly evolving and changing the way organizations and individuals work. SNHU's foundational 3-course series on AI is designed for non-technical users in modern work environments, providing a beginner-friendly gateway to AI without requiring prior AI or computer science knowledge. They’re ideal for business professionals, managers, frontline workers and those with a personal interest in AI who wish to integrate knowledge of AI into their skillset.

AI Foundations Badge Courses Include:
  • Course 1: Introduction to AI Literacy (IT-215)
  • Course 2: Introduction to Responsible AI (IT-216)
  • Course 3: AI In Practice (IT-217)

Note: Courses should be taken in order.

Course 1: Introduction to AI Literacy (IT-215) Overview

In this course, students will gain a comprehensive understanding of artificial intelligence (AI), its practical applications, and ethical considerations. Students will learn to communicate key AI concepts, discern AI facts from misconceptions, and assess generative AI tools. They will explore responsible AI principles, understand AI's societal impacts, and develop skills in creating effective AI prompts, enhancing their literacy in AI's diverse landscape.

  • Define key terminology related to AI such as machine learning, neural networks, narrow AI, general AI, and natural language processing using plain language
  • Compare AI capabilities to human capabilities
  • Differentiate between AI and generative AI
  • Describe the practical applications of AI large language models in various fields
  • Differentiate between AI misconceptions and facts
  • Investigate potential AI applications in various contexts

  • Choose AI tools based on their appropriate usage
  • Describe the benefits and limitations of generative AI applications
  • Identify use cases that could benefit from generative AI solutions
  • Identify generative AI tools and techniques relevant to a specific use case
  • Justify choosing particular generative AI solutions, techniques, or application
  • Compare different prompting strategies for efficacy
  • Assess the effectiveness of a given generative AI solution
  • Engineer introductory level prompts for a given AI application

  • Identify foundational principles of responsible AI
  • Identify potential risks, biases, and societal consequences of AI development and adoption
  • Discuss the potential impact of AI applications across different industries
  • Explore proposed strategies for the adoption of responsible AI

Course 2: Introduction to Responsible AI (IT-216) Overview

In this course, students explore the principles of responsible AI. Students examine practical case studies, identify unethical AI practices, and understand their impacts. They focus on creating and applying ethical guidelines to the practical application of AI and learning to identify and manage risks. The course empowers students to communicate and address complex ethical issues in AI clearly and effectively.

  • Explain core ethical principles relevant to AI systems
  • Discuss the relationship between generative AI use and ethics
  • Identify unethical use of AI, based on an existing framework
  • Analyze AI case studies for ethical compliance based on an existing framework
  • Critique existing AI policies and guidelines from organizations and governments
  • Reflect on AI's ethical implications

  • Explore instances and types of unethical AI use
  • Identify unethical aspects of AI use within the case studies via use of a framework
  • Identify common patterns or unique aspects in unethical AI practices across multiple case studies
  • Discuss the implications for future AI development and usage
  • Develop informed recommendations for avoidance of future ethical breaches in AI projects
  • Evaluate the impact of unethical AI use on different stakeholders

  • Develop ethical guidelines or recommendations for AI projects
  • Apply ethical frameworks and guidelines to the development of risk mitigation strategies
  • Communicate ethical issues and their implications to technical and non-technical stakeholders
  • Differentiate between risks and hazards
  • Assess the potential effectiveness of proposed strategies
  • Revise an implementation plan based on feedback or changing circumstances

Course 3: AI In Practice (IT-217) Overview

In this course, students learn to use generative AI in practical challenges and tasks. They will evaluate AI tools for specific tasks and challenges, integrate generative AI into workflows, and address inaccuracies in AI responses. They gain hands-on experience in selecting and testing AI tools, crafting and adjusting prompts, and ensuring AI-generated content aligns with reliable information, enhancing their proficiency in the practical application of AI.

  • Outline key factors to consider when selecting AI tools
  • Choose from a number of possible AI-based tools for a given situation or task
  • Research the various strengths and limitations of available AI tools
  • Assess the effectiveness of an AI tool(s) for a given task or application
  • Evaluate the limitations of an AI-based tool(s) in general and for a specific task or challenge
  • Test AI tools in trail scenarios

  • Identify modifiable parameters within an AI technology's function
  • Identify areas in industry where generative AI can increase efficiency or reduce pain points
  • Author prompts for a given scenario with prompting frameworks
  • Adjust a prompt based on the AIs response to achieve more accurate or relevant outcome
  • Select the best prompting strategy or framework for a given scenario
  • Apply industry best practices to the use of generative AI
  • Integrate generative AI into workflows to enhance outcomes and/or efficiencies
  • Disclose the use of generative AI

  • Define hallucinations in the context of generative AI
  • Describe how and why AI systems can generate hallucinated or irrelevant information
  • Identify reliable sources of information
  • Identify hallucinated or inefficient responses in generative AI content
  • Edit AI-generated content based on alignment with reliable information
  • Iterate prompts to minimize hallucinated or inefficient responses in generative AI content
  • Recognize common anomalies, inaccuracies, or misinformation that may occur when reviewing AI-generated content
  • Review the output of AI-generated content to ensure it meets the initial input
